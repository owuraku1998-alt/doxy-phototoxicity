Systemic toxicity interpretation — For oral doxycycline: systemic toxicity is predicted primarily from plasma drug levels driven by oral dose and renal clearance — not by skin. The skin reaction (phototoxicity) is a separate local process. For topical doxycycline: the skin IS the absorption barrier. Systemic exposure depends on the permeability coefficient (Kp), application site, surface area, skin integrity, and occlusion. PBPK/PBTK models (Zhang 2024; Krumpholz 2025) are the current best tool for prediction but lack doxycycline-specific validation. No model currently allows precise individual systemic risk prediction from topical application.
